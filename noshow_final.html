Introduction¶

In this report we analyse the No-Show Appointments dataset. The dataset contains information on approximately 110000 mediacal appointments in Brazil and is focused on the question of whether or not patients showed up for their appointment.

The dataset was downloaded from this location

With the analyses in this report I want to find out how age and waiting time influence the no-show rate:

  1. Age related influence
    • Does the patient's age have an influence on the now-show rate?
  2. Waiting time
    • Does the no-show rate depend on the waiting time, i.e. the number of days between the appointment and the day the appointment was scheduled?
  3. SMS
    • For some appointments, SMS are sent. Do the SMS reduce the no-show rate?
  4. Combined effect of SMS and waiting time

def sample_no_show(df, sample_size, n):
    """Take n samples of size sample_size from the 'no-show' column of dataframe df,
    compute the mean of each sample, and return the means as a pandas Series."""
    means = list()
    for i in range(n):
        means.append(df["no-show"].sample(sample_size).mean())
    return pd.Series(means)

Conclusions¶

This analysis investigated the influence of age, waiting time, and SMS on the rate of no-show appointments.

The overall no-show rate is 20%.

Findings are:

  1. The no-show rate is unevenly distributed. Some age groups (ranging from approximately 5 to 30 years) exhibit a no-show rate higher than average (25%), while other age groups are below average. A statistical analysis has been performed on one age group, indicating that this observation is more than pure chance.

  2. I analysed how the no-show rate varies with waiting time (difference in days between the day of the appointment and the day the appointment was scheduled). For waiting time zero (appointment on the same day), the no-show ratee is approximately 5%. For waiting times of 1 day or more, With increasing waiting time, the
    no-show rates are higher, ranging from 23 to 33%.

  3. The no-show rate for appointments that received an SMS is 28%, while it is 17% for appointments without SMS. This observation was surprising and led to a deeper investigation of SMS combined with waiting time.

  4. The data suggests that SMS are sent only if the waiting time is at least three days.

  5. Looking at appointments with waiting time of at least three days only, we can observe that the no-show rate is lower for appointments for which an SMS has been sent.
